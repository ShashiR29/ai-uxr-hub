Evaluating AI Outputs

Frameworks for assessing AI quality, reliability, and fit-for-purpose — critical for researchers who use or study AI-assisted products.

Mini Playbook: Evaluating AI Outputs

🕐 When to Use This Knowledge

  • Selecting AI tools for integration into research workflows
  • Designing studies to measure AI output quality perception
  • Auditing AI-generated research artefacts before sharing
  • Building organisational guidelines for AI use in research
  • Understanding when AI commentary/analysis is reliable

🔑 Key Considerations

  • AI has a sycophancy bias — it will confirm your hypotheses
  • Benchmark scores ≠ real-world task performance
  • The "jagged frontier" means capabilities are uneven and unpredictable
  • 95% of AI commentary misrepresents underlying research
  • Human judgment is still required for context-dependent evaluation

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Trusting AI summaries without verifying against the source
  • Using a model because it's the latest release, not because it fits the task
  • Evaluating AI quality once and never re-evaluating after updates
  • Measuring quality by fluency rather than accuracy or completeness
  • Ignoring edge cases where AI confidently produces wrong outputs
