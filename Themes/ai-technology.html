AI Technology

Understanding how LLMs, context windows, and AI systems work — so you can design better research questions about them.

Mini Playbook: AI Technology Literacy

🕐 When to Use This Knowledge

  • Designing studies on user mental models of AI
  • Explaining AI capabilities and limitations to participants
  • Interpreting why participants trust or distrust AI outputs
  • Bridging communication between product teams and researchers
  • Scoping what AI features are technically feasible to research

🔑 Key Considerations

  • LLMs are "blurry JPEG" models — they approximate, not remember
  • Context windows are finite — long context ≠ perfect recall
  • AI capabilities are "jagged" — expect surprising strengths and weaknesses
  • The Bitter Lesson: scale + data consistently beats clever algorithms
  • Multi-agent systems are the near-future direction, not single models

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Treating LLM outputs as factual retrieval rather than synthesis/generation
  • Expecting consistent outputs — LLMs are probabilistic
  • Using AI capabilities today as a baseline for future research
  • Confusing machine consciousness with behavioural mimicry
  • Ignoring training cutoff dates when interpreting AI knowledge gaps
