Trust & Safety in AI

Understanding how trust forms (and breaks) with AI systems — critical knowledge for designing responsible AI experiences.

Mini Playbook: Trust & Safety

Structured guidance for applying trust & safety knowledge in UX research

🕐 When to Use This Knowledge

  • Evaluating participant reactions to AI-generated content
  • Designing studies that measure AI trust and reliance
  • Assessing safety risks in AI-assisted product features
  • Investigating user mental health impacts of AI tools
  • Reviewing AI ethics in study design and recruitment

🔑 Key Considerations

  • Trust is not binary — it exists on a spectrum and varies by task type
  • AI systems can create parasocial relationships; design with this in mind
  • Vulnerable user populations face amplified risks (mental health, dependency)
  • Institutional trust and interpersonal trust work differently in AI contexts
  • Regulation shapes user expectations — stay current on policy developments

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Conflating "users like it" with "users appropriately trust it"
  • Ignoring long-term dependency effects in short-term usability studies
  • Not distinguishing between algorithm aversion and appropriate scepticism
  • Treating consent as sufficient for safety in AI studies
  • Missing subtle signs of over-reliance in participant behaviour
