AI & Education

How AI is reshaping learning, skill development, and institutional education — and what it means for how researchers develop expertise.

Mini Playbook: AI & Education Research

🕐 When to Use This Knowledge

  • Studying how students and professionals learn with AI assistance
  • Researching AI cheating detection and academic integrity
  • Exploring how AI changes skill acquisition in professional contexts
  • Evaluating AI-augmented learning products and features
  • Understanding generational differences in AI adoption

🔑 Key Considerations

  • Learning with AI ≠ learning — active retrieval still matters
  • AI tutoring can accelerate learning 2x, but requires careful design
  • Students who use AI as a crutch may not develop deep expertise
  • Academic integrity policies are rapidly evolving
  • Different generations have fundamentally different attitudes to AI assistance

⚠️ Common Mistakes

  • Treating AI-assisted work as equivalent to unaided competence
  • Ignoring the neuroscience of memory when evaluating AI study tools
  • Assuming AI improves all types of learning equally
  • Not distinguishing between AI as a scaffold vs AI as a replacement
  • Missing the long-term capability development implications in user studies
