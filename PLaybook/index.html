🛡️ Trust in AI

📚 Start here

AI and Trust — Bruce Schneier ↗ LLMs Try to Please You — and That's a Problem Can AI Chatbots Worsen Psychosis? — Psychology Today ↗ → See all Trust & Safety articles

🚀 What UXRs can explore next

🔬 Run a study

Design a trust calibration study: show users AI outputs of varying quality and measure whether they can tell good from bad. How does trust shift after AI makes a visible mistake?

📄 Read the research

Does the Whole Exceed its Parts? (CHI 2021) — on how people calibrate trust in AI systems. Also: Anthropic's Sleeper Agents paper on deceptive AI.

⚡ Try in your product

Add a "confidence indicator" to an AI feature in your product and test whether it changes user trust behaviour. Even a lightweight A/B test on wording ("I'm not sure" vs. no disclaimer) reveals trust dynamics.

💡 Project idea

Map the "trust journey" for your product's AI features: where do new users start, when does trust peak, and what triggers a trust breakdown? Build a trust lifecycle diagram for your team.

🤝 Human–AI Collaboration

📚 Start here

The Cybernetic Teammate — Ethan Mollick On Working with Wizards ↗ Six Mental Models for Working with AI ↗ → See all Human + AI Workflow articles

🚀 What UXRs can explore next

🔬 Run a study

Observe how people actually hand off work to AI: when do they delegate fully, when do they co-create, and when do they override? Map the "centaur" vs. "cyborg" patterns in your product.

📄 Read the research

Navigating the Jagged Technological Frontier (Harvard/BCG 2023) — the landmark study on Centaur vs. Cyborg work patterns with AI.

⚡ Experiment in VS Code

Use GitHub Copilot for a week on a real task (e.g., building a prototype or writing a script). Journal your experience: when did you accept suggestions? When did you reject? What surprised you? Share findings with the team.

💡 Project idea

Create a "collaboration patterns" taxonomy for your product: categorise the different ways users work with your AI features. Use it as a framework for future studies and design reviews.

🔍 Evaluating AI Outputs

📚 Start here

Giving Your AI a Job Interview LLMs Try to Please You — sycophancy bias The Shape of AI: Jaggedness, Bottlenecks and Salients ↗ → See all Evaluating AI Outputs articles

🚀 What UXRs can explore next

🔬 Run a study

Give participants AI-generated research summaries (some accurate, some subtly wrong) and observe: can they spot errors? What do they trust? This reveals how your users judge AI quality in practice.

📄 Read the research

How is ChatGPT's behaviour changing over time? (Stanford 2023) — shows AI performance isn't stable, which matters for evaluation design. Also: Why 95% of AI Commentary Fails.

⚡ Experiment yourself

Take a recent study you completed. Run the same data through an AI tool (e.g., paste transcripts into Copilot). Compare its themes to your original findings. Where does it add value? Where does it hallucinate?

💡 Project idea

Build a lightweight "AI output scorecard" for your team: 5 criteria to check before trusting an AI-generated artefact. Pilot it on 3 projects and refine. Share as a team standard.

💼 AI Adoption & Behaviour Change

📚 Start here

CEOs Want to Adopt AI — But Do They Get It? ↗ The Bitter Lesson vs The Garbage Can ↗ How Behavioral Science Can Improve AI ROI (HBR) ↗ → See all Future of Work articles

🚀 What UXRs can explore next

🔬 Run a study

Interview power users AND non-adopters of an AI feature side by side. What separates them? Is it workflow fit, trust, awareness, or something else? Map the adoption barriers and enablers.

📄 Read the research

Impact of AI on Developer Productivity (Microsoft Research) — rigorous study on whether AI tools actually change behaviour and output.

⚡ Try at Microsoft

Shadow 3 colleagues who use Copilot daily and 3 who don't. Watch their workflows. What moments trigger reaching for AI vs. doing it manually? Build a "moments that matter" map.

💡 Project idea

Propose a diary study with your product's users: ask them to log every time they use (or deliberately avoid) an AI feature for one week. Analyse the patterns. Share with your PM as input to the adoption strategy.

🧪 AI Tools for UXR Practice

📚 Start here

Learn to Use AI Like a Surgeon ↗ Making AI Work: Leadership, Lab, and Crowd ↗ The Cybernetic Teammate → See all AI Technology articles

🚀 What UXRs can explore next

⚡ Experiment in VS Code

Build something small with GitHub Copilot: a research dashboard, a participant screener, an automated analysis script. You don't need to be a developer — just describe what you want in plain English and iterate.

🔬 Run a self-study

Use AI for your next analysis (theme generation, quote tagging, insight synthesis). Document where it helps and where it hallucinates. Share learnings in a team brown-bag session.

📄 Explore further

Microsoft Office of Applied Research — see how Microsoft researchers are using AI in practice. Also explore: GitHub Copilot docs for what's possible.

💡 Project idea

Create a "UXR AI toolkit" document for your team: 5 specific tasks where AI works well, 3 where it doesn't, and the prompts/tools to use. Keep it practical and opinionated. Update quarterly.
