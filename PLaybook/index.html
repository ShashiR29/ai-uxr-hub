🔍 Evaluating AI Outputs

What It Is

A systematic approach to assessing whether AI-generated content is accurate, complete, appropriately scoped, and fit for research use — going beyond surface plausibility to verify quality.

When to Use It

Whenever AI tools are used to synthesise interview data, generate themes, summarise findings, or produce any research artefact that will influence decisions.

Time Investment

15–30 min per major AI-generated artefact for spot-check validation. Build it into your research process as standard practice — not an optional audit.

Step-by-Step Framework

  1. Define your quality bar first — before generating anything, specify what "good" looks like for this specific task.
  2. Use the job interview approach — test the AI on 3 representative tasks (easy, medium, hard) before relying on it for real work.
  3. Adversarial prompting — ask the model to challenge its own output: "What are the weaknesses of this analysis?" or "What did I miss?"
  4. Source verification — for every major insight, trace it back to at least one source quote or data point.
  5. Blind validation — have a team member evaluate the AI output without knowing which insights came from AI vs. human analysis.
  6. Document what was AI-generated — maintain a transparent record for research accountability.

🤖 Copilot Prompts

Critically evaluate this research synthesis: [paste output]. Identify: (1) claims that are not supported by the data, (2) themes that seem too broad or overlapping, (3) perspectives that may be missing, and (4) any signs of sycophancy toward the researcher's framing.
I used AI to generate these 5 research themes: [paste themes]. For each theme, give me: the strongest counterargument, one alternative interpretation, and a question a sceptical stakeholder might ask.

⚡ Designing Copilot Experiences

What It Is

Research methods and frameworks for understanding how users perceive, adopt, and work with AI-powered Copilot features — from first exposure to habitual use.

When to Use It

When designing user studies for Copilot, AI assistant, or embedded AI feature evaluations. Also applicable when testing AI suggestions, completions, and proactive AI behaviours.

Key Research Questions

Does the user understand what AI is doing and why? Does the AI suggestion help or interrupt? How is trust calibrated over repeated interactions?

Research Design Principles

  • Study mental models early — participants' prior expectations of AI shape their entire experience. Capture these before exposure.
  • Longitudinal beats snapshot — trust in AI Copilot features evolves over time; single-session studies miss reliance patterns and calibration.
  • Observe the "handoff moment" — when do users accept, modify, or reject AI suggestions? This reveals trust calibration.
  • Capture both hits and misses — only observing successful AI moments creates a false picture. Structure tasks to surface AI failures.
  • Centaur performance benchmark — measure performance when AI is used (Centaur) vs. without AI (human-only) to understand genuine value.
  • Study diverse user groups — AI cynics and AI over-trusters both represent critical failure modes; recruit for range.

🤖 Copilot Prompts

I'm designing a user study to evaluate a Copilot feature that [describe feature]. Generate a 6-question discussion guide that explores: mental model of AI, trust calibration, moments of acceptance vs rejection of suggestions, and long-term reliance concerns. Tailor questions for a 45-minute moderated session.
Here are 8 participant quotes from a Copilot usability study: [paste quotes]. Identify patterns around: (1) trust signals and triggers, (2) moments of over-reliance, (3) moments of under-use, and (4) any unmet needs. Do not add interpretations not supported by the quotes.

🛡️ Measuring Trust in AI

What It Is

A structured approach to measuring how users form, calibrate, and lose trust in AI systems — distinguishing between appropriate trust, over-reliance, and algorithm aversion.

When to Use It

When studying any AI feature where user reliance matters: AI recommendations, AI-generated content, AI-assisted decisions, or autonomous AI agents acting on behalf of users.

Trust Dimensions

Competence trust (can it do the task?), benevolence trust (does it act in my interest?), integrity trust (is it honest?), and predictability (will it behave consistently?).

Measurement Framework

  • Baseline trust survey — measure general AI trust attitudes before any product exposure (use validated scales: TPA, TILLTA, or Semantic Differential).
  • Behavioural trust proxies — observe: does the user act on AI recommendations without verification? Do they modify AI outputs before using them?
  • Trust calibration test — present AI outputs of known varying quality and measure whether participants correctly identify good vs. poor outputs.
  • Over-reliance detection — present plausible-but-wrong AI outputs and measure acceptance rate.
  • Algorithm aversion probe — present identical advice attributed to "an AI" vs. "a human expert" and measure differential trust.
  • Longitudinal trust drift — measure trust at week 1, week 4, and week 8 to capture attrition vs. habituation patterns.

⚠️ Watch Out For

Trust can be simultaneously too high (over-reliance) and too low (under-use) in the same user — they may blindly accept AI suggestions on familiar tasks and refuse AI help on unfamiliar ones. Design studies to capture both failure modes.

🤖 Copilot Prompts

I'm designing a study to measure trust in an AI recommendation feature for [product]. Generate a mixed-methods measurement plan that includes: a pre-study survey instrument (5–7 questions covering trust dimensions), 3 behavioural observation tasks, and 4 debrief questions to explore trust reasoning. Include specific wording I can use directly.
Based on these participant observations from a trust study: [paste observations]. Classify each instance as: appropriate trust, over-reliance, under-reliance, or algorithm aversion. For each classification, cite the specific behaviour that informed your judgment.

🔬 Using AI in Research Workflows

What It Is

A principled framework for integrating AI into UX research activities — deciding where AI adds value, where human judgment is non-negotiable, and how to maintain research rigour throughout.

When to Use It

When setting up a new research project, onboarding team members to AI-assisted research, or building organisational guidelines for responsible AI use in research practice.

The Centaur Model

The most effective AI research approach: researcher directs AI strategy and exercises final judgment; AI handles breadth tasks (drafting, enumerating, organising). Neither solo is as effective.

AI Use by Research Phase

Research Phase Good AI Uses ✅ Keep Human ❌
Planning Draft discussion guides, suggest research questions, enumerate potential user groups Formulating the core research question, defining success criteria
Data Collection Auto-transcription, real-time note suggestions, stimulus generation Interviewing participants, building rapport, probing responses
Analysis Initial theme enumeration, quote extraction, pattern flagging Interpreting context, validating themes, deciding what matters
Reporting Draft executive summaries, slide structures, recommendation lists Prioritising findings, framing for stakeholders, making recommendations
Repository Tagging, categorising, summarising past studies Deciding relevance of past research to new contexts

🤖 Copilot Prompts

I'm planning a user study on [topic] for a [product type] team. My research question is: [RQ]. Draft a 45-minute moderated interview guide with: (1) warm-up questions, (2) scenario-based tasks, (3) probing questions for each task, and (4) closing questions. Flag any areas where I should probe further based on common research biases.
Here are transcripts from 5 user interviews on [topic]: [paste transcripts]. Enumerate all distinct themes present in the data. For each theme: give it a descriptive name, provide 2 supporting quotes, and rate how many participants mentioned it (n=5). Do not interpret — just catalogue what's there.
